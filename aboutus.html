Our Histrory

To the greatest benefit to humankind since 1900

The Nobel Prize has been a symbol of recognition for outstanding contributions to science, humanism, and peace since its inception. Rooted in the will of Alfred Nobel, the prize is designed to reward those whose work has significantly advanced the welfare of humanity. This guiding principle not only defines the Nobel Prize but also informs the extensive outreach efforts aimed at engaging and inspiring the public. These activities serve to spread knowledge about the Nobel Prize and the remarkable discoveries and achievements of its laureates. Established on 29 June 1900, the Nobel Prize has its foundations in Sweden and Norway, with the prestigious award ceremonies held annually on 10 December.

Nobel Prize nominations

Nomination of Nobel Prize laureates

The nomination process for Nobel Prize laureates is a meticulous and confidential procedure designed to ensure the selection of the most deserving individuals and organizations. Nominations are made by a select group of experts and distinguished individuals, each chosen for their knowledge and expertise in the relevant fields. This careful process preserves the integrity and prestige of the Nobel Prize, ensuring that those who have made the most significant contributions to humanity are recognized and celebrated.
